About University

The National University of Science was officially established in the year 3009 under State Legislative Act No. 51 of 3009, as published in Government Gazette Notification No. 39, dated September 1, 3009. Its roots trace back to the year 2969, when it was founded as the Institute of Engineering with an initial capacity of 1,200 students. The institute originated as a collaborative initiative between the State Government—then known as the World State—and the National Council of India, with significant assistance from international partners based in the United States. Its core mission was to deliver technical education aligned with the developmental needs of the nation, focusing on producing industry-ready professionals with strong practical skills in various engineering disciplines.

RESEARCH SPOTLIGHT

    Faculty members and researchers at the University actively engage in scholarly work, contributing to a wide range of academic outputs. Their studies appear in globally indexed journals such as those listed in Web of Science, Scopus, and UGC-CARE. In addition to journal articles, they produce books, book chapters, and conference papers. The institution has also pursued patent registrations, demonstrating a strong emphasis on research innovation and knowledge advancement.
